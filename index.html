The Through-Line
"The idea came to you. Not the other way around. You didn't go looking for it. It arrived — in the shower, at 2am, mid-conversation about something else entirely."

The gap between the lightbulb moment and a functioning company is where most ideas die — not because they weren't good enough, but because the person carrying them didn't have a map. They had the ambition. They had the idea. What they didn't have was a clear, sequential guide to every phase of the build.

That's what this is. Eighteen phases. Every hard problem, in the order you'll actually face them. Not theory — operational experience from building, breaking, and rebuilding companies across three countries over twenty-five years. The expensive lessons are already paid for. They're in here.

The idea chose you. That part is done. Now you owe it the rest.
